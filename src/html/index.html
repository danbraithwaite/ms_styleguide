    • Introduction

    The goal of this style guide is to document the current design patterns used across M&S.com and our apps, in the hope of providing direction and improving consistency in the design and development process.

    This isnâ€™t a definitive/set-in-stone approach to designing for M&S.com, but rather a foundation for future work. Elements in this guide have been gathered from existing patterns and therefor will no doubt evolve. Keep in mind however that updating or adding elements to this guide will require a case for doing so. Adding in new elements comes at a cost both in design and in development.

    The style guide has been loosely based on the principles of Atomic Design. Our style guide consists of three levels; Globals, Components and Modules.

    Globals are elements that are found on every single M&S.com page and can be boiled down to three simple pieces; Colour Palette, Typography and the Grid. Each of these is vital in building any Component or Module.

    Components are heavily influenced by the Global style, with small elements being put together to make a complete component. An example of this could be a button, where the button colour is defined by the colour palette and type style and size also being defined by our typography rules - both of which are found in our Global section.

    Modules are made up of various Components to create a large content block. Modules can range from a Sign in form, to a 4 column image layout for a campaign page. For a form module, there will need to be buttons, text fields, drop downs, radio buttons etc - all of which are defined in our Components section.
